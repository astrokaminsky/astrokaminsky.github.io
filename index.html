Hello! My name is Aidan Kaminsky and I am currently a PhD candidate at the University of Miami. I work under the supervision of Dr. Nico Cappelluti, where I study the Cosmic Infrared and X-ray Background (CIB and CXB) anisotropies in order to understand the cosmic star formation history and supermassive black hole formation. Star formation and supermassive black hole growth are related to their large-scale environments, and these physical connections are imprinted in both of these backgrounds. I use theoretical models and data from the James Webb Space Telescope, Spitzer, Chandra, and Euclid.

Before beginning my PhD work, I spent a year studying under Postdoctoral fellow Lars Bonne at the SOFIA Science Center, where I modelled the dynamics of the Musca filamentary cloud, a pristine string-like structure in the interstellar medium that is early in its gravitational fragmentation. While these projects seem (and are!) very different, the connection lies in the physics of star formation. What physics processes drive the evolution of star formation? How do large-scale structures, supermassive black hole feedback, etc. affect such processes? How did the physics of star formation look like in the early Universe? These are all questions that I am interested in addressing. This page is dedicated to past and on-going projects. Enjoy!
